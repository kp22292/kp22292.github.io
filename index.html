This may seem odd coming from a high school student, but I believe my past experiences are just as important as my future training in helping me achieve my goals. I was also on the design team so I got to help design the robot in regards to how it would function and score points. In addition, by participating in an engineering camp through the National Student Leadership Conference, I was able to work with electronics and create smaller robots. I was able to use my knowledge from Science Olympiad and my physics classes to create a trebuchet using six feet of PVC pipe and a dowel rod. Finally, through activities such as Boy Scouts of America, Taekwondo, Speech and Debate, and Model Atlanta Regional Commission, I have been able to work on my public speaking skills, various outdoor skills, and leadership skills that will help me work with my team/group better and more efficiently.

Although past events may be important, the education and training I will receive through a Petroleum Engineering program will help me reach my goal. I will be able to learn the properties of fossil fuels. I will be able to learn about the earth and grasp a better understanding of geology. I will be able to continue learning calculus and will be able to learn more about physics than I have in high school. I will be able to learn about material science. I will be able to enhance my knowledge on thermodynamics and fluids. I will be able to learn about drills and rigs and how they work. The knowledge from a Petroleum Engineering program is endless and my experiences and advancements towards my goal will be the same.

Knowing that I am now at a stage where my parents and teachers do not need to tell me what my goals are is a liberating experience. I can make my own decisions and I know I am making the right ones, especially regarding my decision to be a Petroleum Engineer.
